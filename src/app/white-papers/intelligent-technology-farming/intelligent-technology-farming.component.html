How the Use of Intelligent Technologies in Farming shapes the Future of the Agriculture Industry

by Shashika Heshan

Agriculture (Farming) is considered as one of the oldest and indispensable industries of humanity, where it initially began in a traditional and conventional way utilizing manual means that farmers used to dig and plant by hand. Now the farming has progressed on to the modernized farms where it uses mechanized, technological and innovative approaches such as tractors, harvesters and digital management procedures, etc. to feed the growing number of world population. However, with the rapidly increasing world population, these technological approaches, alone, will not suffice to produce nutritional food crops with an optimal efficiency to fulfil the incrementing global food demand. In this paper we will further elaborate on intelligent technologies, such as Internet of Things (IOT), precision farming, sensors and mobility, etc., which could be used to gain and reap higher yields with better efficiency.

What is Intelligent Farming?


Intelligent Farming is a concept of utilizing modern technologies in farming, such as IOT, GPS, Drones, Sensors and Analytics, etc., to boost the productivity, quality and quantity of agricultural farming products. At present, a farmer is not just a person who has a strong interest in agriculture, he has to acquire data and knowledge on how the market behaves and price changes, in order to produce and sell out the agricultural products to satisfy the demand while increasing the profits. Hence implementing Intelligent Farming techniques, for an instance, sensors implanted in a field will gather various kinds of data on soil, like temperature, acidity and soil nutrients. With the precise information on the resources in the field, farmers could be well equipped to carry out different strategies to each field to apply fertilizers, irrigation and pesticides to effectively gain better yields with high nutritional value. As the world population continues to grow, intelligent farming becomes prominent as a new tendency that will allow agriculture industry to be more productive and profitable.

Challenges faced by Agricultural Industry:


Nowadays, with the vast changing world, the Agriculture Industry faces a number of challenges; it might be economic based issues and non-economic issues. The agriculture industry can be considered as a resource heavy industry. Most of the agriculture businesses heavily depend on manual work processes, hence they have been found wanting to manage daily operations and produce agricultural products against the increasing number of demands amidst the competitors. Below are some challenges that Agriculture Industry comes across as the world continues to evolve.

➊ Feeding the world population which keeps growing and to satisfy demanding global food supply:
Studies have already found that agriculture has to deliver more agricultural products to compensate the rising number of demands due to the growing global population, which is supposed to rise to over 9.6 billion (Creating a Sustainable Food Future: Interim Findings, 2013) by the year 2050. This presents a huge challenge for the Agriculture Industry, as to provide quality food to satisfy the demanding global food supply. The projections also revealed that it would need around 70% more food production (How to feed the World in 2050, 2009) than today to meet the needs of the world population by 2050. Hence it is imperative to state that the agriculture industry needs to adopt effective smart technological approaches which could intensify and boost the production of agricultural farming products to satisfy the demanding global food supply projected over the next couple of decades.

➋ Reduce the amount of losses and waste while increasing the production of agricultural products:
Studies have shown that nearly around half of the consumer waste (Creating a Sustainable Food Future: Interim Findings, 2013) in developed countries takes the food loss and waste, while in developing countries there is a 2/3 of food loss (Creating a Sustainable Food Future: Interim Findings, 2013) occurring during agriculture processes such as harvesting, handling and lastly due to improper storage of agricultural products (Food). Along the way, implementing steps to minimize the food loss and waste that take place during the farming procedures will unarguably feed more people, therefore the food gap will get lessen. Adhering to proper farm management practices along with intelligent technologies will pave the way to mitigate the issue.

➌ Conserve natural environmental resources whilst gaining higher yields with better efficiency:
Researches have been carried out and found that, as of now, yielding of agricultural products account for emission of 13% of world’s greenhouse gases and about 6.5 gig tons of CO2e (Carbon dioxide equivalent) per year (Creating a Sustainable Food Future: Interim Findings, 2013). Further OECD (Organization for Economic Co-operation and Development) states that agriculture consumes 70% of world’s fresh water (Water and Agriculture, OECD) and that being said water is an inevitable resource that relates to agriculture. However, with the need to meet the demanding global food supply, agriculture industry needs to take remedial actions to mitigate and reduce use of excessive amount of fertilizers and pesticides, which hampers the preservation of environmental resources. With Intelligent Farming, it could broadly lessen the unnecessary use of materials in fields with the help of proper and accurate analytics.

➍ Increase of demand for higher nutritional value food:
At present, gradually people are shifting to healthy and active lifestyles. They are already adopting a change in their eating habits by shifting from processed food to healthy organic eating habits. The growth of demands for natural organic food with high nutrition value over processed and chemical laced food has increased dramatically over the years. Hence the agriculture industry should be capable of efficiently producing agricultural products with higher nutritional value to meet the rapidly increasing demand from the global population.
